Once upon a build, in a website tucked behind a friendly green checkmark, there lived a careful little wizard named Zer0.

Zer0 counted everything. Every heading, every link, every semicolon — twice on Fridays.

But on the night before the big launch, Zer0 counted the pixels on the home page and gasped. One million, forty-eight thousand, five hundred and seventy-*five*. One pixel was missing!

{% include components/book-plate.html src="/assets/images/wizard-on-journey.png" alt="Zer0 the wizard sets off along a winding path at dusk, satchel glowing" %}

"A site with a hole in it is no site of mine," said Zer0, packing a satchel with a spare stylesheet and a thermos of hot cocoa.

The trail of the missing pixel led out of the tidy home page, through the footer, and up into the wild Mountains of Markdown, where the headings grow larger the higher you climb.

Zer0 walked and walked, past sleeping code blocks and a very polite table that said "excuse me" in every cell.

And there, at the top of the tallest heading, the trail simply stopped.

"Hmm," said Zer0, sipping the cocoa. "Every mystery is just a page that hasn't loaded yet."
